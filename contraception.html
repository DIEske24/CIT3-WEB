Contraception Methods

Contraception, also known as birth control, refers to methods used to prevent pregnancy. Some methods also protect against sexually transmitted infections (STIs). Choosing the right contraceptive depends on personal preferences, health, and lifestyle.

Types of Contraception:

  • Barrier Methods: Prevent sperm from reaching the egg.
    • Male condoms: A thin sheath worn over the penis to prevent sperm from entering the vagina. Also protects against STIs.
    • Female condoms: A pouch inserted into the vagina to block sperm.
    • Diaphragm & Cervical Cap: Dome-shaped devices placed inside the vagina before intercourse to block sperm.
  • Hormonal Methods: Use hormones to prevent ovulation or change the uterine lining.
    • Birth control pills: Taken daily to prevent ovulation.
    • Contraceptive patch: A small patch placed on the skin that releases hormones.
    • Birth control shot (Depo-Provera): An injection given every three months.
    • Vaginal ring: A flexible ring placed in the vagina that releases hormones.
    • Hormonal IUD: A device inserted into the uterus that releases hormones to prevent pregnancy.
  • Long-Acting Reversible Contraception (LARC): Highly effective, low-maintenance methods.
    • Intrauterine Device (IUD): A small device placed in the uterus. Can be hormonal or copper-based (non-hormonal).
    • Contraceptive Implant: A small rod placed under the skin that releases hormones.
  • Emergency Contraception: Used after unprotected sex to prevent pregnancy.
    • Emergency contraception pill (Plan B, Ella): Taken within 72 hours after intercourse.
    • Copper IUD: Can be inserted within 5 days to prevent pregnancy.
  • Permanant Methods: Surgical procedures for individuals who no longer want children.
    • Vasectomy: A surgical procedure that cuts or seals the tubes carrying sperm.
    • Tubal ligation ("getting tubes tied"): A procedure that closes or blocks the fallopian tubes.

How to Choose the Right Method

When selecting contraception, consider effectiveness, convenience, side effects, and whether you want protection against STIs. Consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.
